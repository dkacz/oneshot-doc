# Family Glossary Format (`GLOSSARY.md`)

The glossary is opinionated: for each concept it chooses one canonical term and lists terms to avoid. It is a glossary and nothing else; framing decisions go into `decisions/`, document content goes into documents.

```markdown
# Glossary: <family name>

<One or two sentences on what this document family is and who it serves.>

## Terms

**<canonical term>**
<One or two definition sentences. What it IS, not what it does.>
_Avoid_: <term1>, <term2>

**<canonical term>**
...
_Avoid_: ...
```

## Rules

- **Be decisive.** When several words circulate for one concept, choose the best one and put the rest under "Avoid".
- **Keep definitions tight.** Maximum two sentences.
- **Only family-specific terms.** General concepts (paragraph, bibliography, multiplier in the textbook sense) do not enter unless the family gives them a specific settled meaning.
- **Update at the moment of settlement.** A term settled in the interview goes into the glossary immediately, not after delivery.
- **Group under subheadings** when natural clusters emerge; a flat list is fine for small glossaries.
